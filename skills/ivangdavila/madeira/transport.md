# Madeira Transport Guide

## Overview

Madeira is a small island (57km x 22km) but mountainous terrain means driving takes longer than distances suggest. Public transport exists but is Funchal-centric. Most visitors and residents find a car essential for exploring.

---

## Public Transport

### Buses (Horários do Funchal)

**In Funchal:**
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Coverage | Good within city and suburbs |
| Frequency | Every 10-30 min on main routes |
| Fare | €1.95 per ride (cash on bus) |
| Day pass | €5.35 |
| Monthly pass | €35 (Funchal), €45 (regional) |
| App | Horários do Funchal |

**To Other Towns:**
| Route | Frequency | Time | Notes |
|-------|-----------|------|-------|
| Funchal → Caniço | Every 20-30 min | 30 min | Good connection |
| Funchal → Machico | Every 30-60 min | 45 min | Via expressway |
| Funchal → Ribeira Brava | Every 30-60 min | 40 min | West coast |
| Funchal → Câmara de Lobos | Every 15-20 min | 20 min | Frequent |
| Funchal → Porto Moniz | 2-3 per day | 2+ hours | Limited |
| Funchal → Santana | 3-4 per day | 1h 15min | Limited |

**Reality**: Buses work for Funchal and main towns. For north coast, mountains, and flexible exploring — you need a car.

### Taxis

| Type | Details |
|------|---------|
| Availability | Good in Funchal, main towns |
| Finding | Taxi ranks, call, apps |
| Pricing | Metered or fixed rates to tourist spots |
| Airport → Funchal | €25-35 (fixed) |

**Tip**: Agree on price for longer trips before starting.

### Uber / Bolt
- **Uber**: Works in Funchal, fewer drivers than mainland
- **Bolt**: Also available
- **Reality**: Not as reliable as major cities; taxis often faster

### Cable Cars

| Route | Purpose | Price |
|-------|---------|-------|
| Funchal → Monte | Tourist (gardens, views) | €12.50 one way, €18 return |
| Monte → Botanical Garden | Connect attractions | €9 one way |

Not transport per se, but useful for reaching Monte area.

## Driving in Madeira

### Road Types

| Road | Character |
|------|-----------|
| Expressways (Via Rápida) | Fast, tunnels, connects main areas |
| Regional roads (ER) | Winding, scenic, slower |
| Mountain roads | Narrow, hairpin curves, dramatic |
| Village streets | Very narrow, one-way systems |

### Key Routes

| Route | Connects | Character |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| VR1 (Via Rápida) | Funchal to airport, Ribeira Brava | Fast, tunnels |
| ER101 | Coastal road (south) | Scenic, winding |
| ER103 | Funchal to Santana | Mountain crossing |
| ER110 | Paul da Serra plateau | High altitude |

### Driving Tips

1. **Hairpin curves**: Hundreds on mountain roads. Go slow, use horn on blind curves.
2. **Tunnels**: Many are narrow, single-lane. Lights required.
3. **Passing**: Wider vehicle yields; not always obvious who should.
4. **Altitude**: Fog can appear suddenly in mountains.
5. **Fuel**: Fill up in Funchal; stations sparse in rural areas.
6. **Parking**: Difficult in Funchal center; use paid lots.

### Specific Challenges

| Route | Challenge |
|-------|-----------|
| Curral das Freiras | Steep, narrow, many curves |
| North coast road | Exposed, can be wet/windy |
| Porto Moniz area | Long drive, curves entire way |
| Pico do Areeiro | Altitude, fog, steep |

### Navigation
- Google Maps works well
- Download offline maps (reception spotty in mountains)
- Some levada trailheads not accurately marked
- Waze also functional

- Located 20km east of Funchal, near Santa Cruz

## Parking

### Funchal

| Area | Parking | Cost |
|------|---------|------|
| Old Town | Very limited | €1-2/hour |
| City center | Street + paid lots | €1-2/hour |
| Hotel zone | Easier, some free | Varies |
| Shopping centers | Free/validated | Usually free |

**Tip**: Use parking garages. Street parking in center is frustrating.

### Elsewhere
- Most villages have free parking
- Levada trailheads usually have parking
- Popular spots (Porto Moniz, 25 Fontes) can fill up in summer

## Getting Around Funchal (No Car)

### By Foot
Central Funchal is very walkable. Old town, center, marina all connected.

**Challenge**: Hills. Funchal is steep. Good shoes essential.

### By Bus
Good network covers:
- Center to hotel zone
- Center to Monte (or use cable car)
- To suburbs (Santo António, São Martinho)
- To nearby towns (Caniço, Câmara de Lobos)

### By Cable Car
- Funchal → Monte: Best views
- Monte → Botanical Garden: Connects attractions

### By Taxi
Plentiful in center. Fixed rates to common destinations posted at ranks.

## Tips by Situation

### Tourist (1 week)
- Rent car for 3-4 days (island exploration)
- Use bus/taxi in Funchal days
- Return car before final Funchal day (avoid parking hassle)

### Digital Nomad (1-3 months)
- Weekly/monthly car rental if outside Funchal
- In Funchal: bus pass + occasional rental
- Consider: electric scooter for short trips

### Resident
- Buy used car (€5,000-12,000) or long-term lease
- Funchal residents can manage without; everyone else needs one
- Factor in maintenance, insurance, annual inspection (IPO)
