# Madeira Gardens & Parks

## Why Gardens?

Madeira's subtropical climate + colonial history created exceptional gardens. British, Portuguese, and other European influences combined with exotic plants from around the world. Some gardens rival the best in Europe.

---

## Must-See Gardens

### Monte Palace Tropical Garden
**The showstopper — don't miss this**

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| Location | Monte, above Funchal |
| Getting there | Cable car from Funchal (15min) or car/taxi |
| Entry | €15 |
| Time needed | 2-3 hours |
| Highlights | Exotic plants, azulejos, oriental collection, koi ponds |

**What to expect**: 70,000+ plant species across themed gardens. Japanese garden, African collection, massive tile (azulejo) displays. Steeply terraced — wear good shoes.

**Combine with**: Toboggan ride down (traditional wicker sled).

**Tips**:
- Morning light best for photos
- Go up by cable car, down by toboggan or walk
- Restaurant on site if needed

### Jardim Botânico (Botanical Garden)
**More scientific, equally beautiful**

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| Location | Hillside above Funchal |
| Getting there | Cable car from Funchal or bus/taxi |
| Entry | €7.50 |
| Time needed | 1.5-2 hours |
| Highlights | Native plants, tropical sections, bird park |

**What to expect**: More organized than Monte Palace. Good views over Funchal. Native Madeiran plant collection. Bird section with parrots.

**Best for**: Those interested in botany, not just beauty.

### Jardins do Palheiro
**English garden with Madeiran twist**

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| Location | Above Funchal (Camacha direction) |
| Getting there | Car/taxi only |
| Entry | €14 |
| Time needed | 1.5-2 hours |
| Highlights | English country garden style, camellia collection, views |

**Character**: Created by British family (Blandy), feels like English country estate crossed with subtropical paradise. Quieter, more refined than Monte Palace.

**Best for**: Garden enthusiasts, those wanting peaceful escape.

## Natural Parks

### Laurisilva Forest (UNESCO)
**Not a garden but essential nature**

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| What | Ancient laurel forest, UNESCO World Heritage |
| Where | Throughout mountains, especially north side |
| Access | Via levada walks |
| Best levadas | 25 Fontes, Caldeirão Verde, Vereda do Fanal |

**Why special**: Largest surviving laurel forest in the world. 20 million years old. Walking through it is like being in a primeval forest.

### Ribeiro Frio Natural Park
**Small nature park with forest trails**

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| Location | Mountains (road to Santana) |
| Entry | Free |
| Highlights | Trout hatchery, short walks, start of Balcões levada |

**For**: Quick stop on mountain drive, easy forest taste.

### Madeira Natural Park
**The whole central spine is protected**

Most of Madeira's interior is protected natural park, covering 2/3 of the island. The levadas run through this protected area.

## When to Visit

### Best Seasons for Flowers

| Month | What's Blooming |
|-------|-----------------|
| Jan-Mar | Camellias, early orchids |
| Apr-May | Everything! Flower festival time |
| Jun-Aug | Subtropical, roses, dahlias |
| Sep-Oct | Late summer flowers |
| Nov-Dec | Poinsettias, Christmas plants |

**Year-round**: Madeira's climate means something is always flowering.

### Flower Festival (Festa da Flor)
Late April/early May. Funchal decorated with flower carpets, parades, displays. Gardens at peak. Book accommodation early.

## Garden Tips

### What to Bring
- Good walking shoes (gardens are hilly)
- Camera
- Sunscreen/hat
- Water
- Cash for entry (some don't take cards)

### Planning
- **Half day**: One major garden (Monte or Botanical)
- **Full day**: Two gardens + lunch
- **Combine with**: Cable car, Monte toboggan, Funchal exploration

### For Photographers
- **Best light**: Morning (before 11am) or late afternoon
- **Tripod**: Allowed in most gardens
- **Macro**: Bring macro lens for flower detail

---

## If Time Is Limited

**Only one garden?** → Monte Palace (most spectacular)

**Two gardens?** → Monte Palace + Botanical Garden (both accessible by cable car)

**Want peace?** → Palheiro (quietest, most refined)

**In Funchal center?** → Quinta das Cruzes (quick, city-center)

## Gardens + Other Activities

### Monte Palace Day
1. Cable car up to Monte
2. Monte Palace gardens (2-3h)
3. Lunch at garden café or nearby
4. Toboggan ride down (or walk)
5. Explore Monte village
6. Cable car or walk to Botanical Garden

### Garden + Levada Day
1. Morning: Easy levada (Balcões - short)
2. Stop at Ribeiro Frio park
3. Afternoon: Palheiro gardens (on way back to Funchal)

### Relaxed Funchal Day
1. Morning: Quinta das Cruzes
2. Walk through city
3. Afternoon: Botanical Garden via cable car
4. Evening: Old town dinner
