# Non-EU Citizens: Visas for Madeira

## Overview

Non-EU citizens need a visa to stay in Portugal (including Madeira) for more than 90 days. Several options exist depending on your situation.

| Visa Type | Best For | Key Requirement |
|-----------|----------|-----------------|
| D7 | Passive income / retirees | Stable income (€760+/month) |
| Digital Nomad Visa | Remote workers | Remote work contract + income |
| D2 | Entrepreneurs | Business plan + capital |
| Work Visa | Employed by Portuguese company | Job offer |
| Student Visa | Students | Enrollment + funds |
| Golden Visa | Investors | Investment (fund-based now) |

---

## Schengen Tourist Entry

### 90/180 Day Rule
- **90 days** in any **180-day period** in Schengen Area
- Includes all Schengen countries (most of EU)
- Cannot extend by leaving and returning

### What You Can Do
- Tourism
- Business meetings
- Scouting for longer-term move
- NOT work (even remote, technically)

### Requirements
- Valid passport (6+ months)
- Proof of accommodation
- Return ticket
- Sufficient funds
- Travel insurance

---

## D7 Visa (Passive Income)

### Overview
The most popular visa for retirees and those with passive income (investments, pensions, remote work income).

### Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Income | €760/month minimum (Portuguese minimum wage) |
| Income type | Passive: pensions, investments, rental income |
| Savings alternative | Show savings equivalent to income requirement |
| Accommodation | Proof of housing (12-month rental contract) |
| Health insurance | Valid in Portugal |
| Clean record | Criminal background check |
| NIF | Portuguese tax number |

### Process

1. **Gather documents** (apostilled/legalized)
2. **Apply at Portuguese consulate** in home country
3. **Wait for approval** (2-4 months)
4. **Enter Portugal** with visa
5. **Apply for residence permit** (SEF/AIMA appointment within 4 months)
6. **Receive residence card** (1-2 year validity initially)

### Costs
- Visa application: ~€90
- Residence permit: ~€150
- Document legalization: Varies
- Legal assistance: €500-2,000 (optional but recommended)

### Timeline
- Preparation: 1-2 months
- Application processing: 2-4 months
- Total to residence card: 4-8 months

## Digital Nomad Visa

### Overview
Introduced 2022, specifically for remote workers employed by non-Portuguese companies.

### Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Income | €3,040/month (4x Portuguese minimum wage) |
| Employment | Contract with company outside Portugal |
| Company | Must have existed 12+ months |
| Work type | Remote work for foreign employer |
| Health insurance | Valid in Portugal |
| Clean record | Criminal background check |

### Key Differences from D7
- **Higher income threshold** (€3,040 vs €760)
- **Must be employed** (not passive income)
- **Employer must be foreign** (not Portuguese company)
- **Faster processing** (in theory)

### Process
Similar to D7:
1. Apply at consulate
2. Enter with visa
3. Apply for residence permit
4. Receive card

## Golden Visa (Investment)

### Current Status (2024)
Real estate route **closed** in 2023. Current options:

| Investment Type | Minimum |
|-----------------|---------|
| Investment funds | €500,000 |
| Capital transfer | €1.5 million |
| Company creation | €500,000 + 5 jobs |
| Research/culture | €250,000-500,000 |

### What It Offers
- Residence permit
- Minimal stay requirement (7 days/year)
- Path to citizenship (5 years)
- Schengen access

### Reality Check
- Expensive
- Complex
- Not for typical nomads/expats
- Consult specialist immigration lawyer

## D2 Visa (Entrepreneurs)

### For
Those starting a business in Portugal.

### Requirements
- Business plan
- Proof of capital
- Relevant experience
- Benefit to Portugal (jobs, innovation)

### Process
- More complex than D7
- May require investment
- Consult lawyer for specifics

## Work Visa

### For
Those with job offer from Portuguese employer.

### Requirements
- Job contract
- Employer sponsors visa
- Position couldn't be filled locally (in some cases)

### Process
- Employer initiates much of the process
- SEF/AIMA approval needed
- Tied to specific employer initially

---

## The Application Process (General)

### Step 1: Gather Documents

Common documents needed:
- Valid passport
- Passport photos
- Proof of income/savings
- Criminal record (apostilled)
- Health insurance
- Accommodation proof
- NIF (can get remotely via lawyer)
- Application forms

**Apostille**: Documents need apostille or legalization for Portugal to accept them.

### Step 2: Consulate Application
- Apply at Portuguese consulate in your country of residence
- Interview may be required
- Processing: 2-4 months

### Step 3: Enter Portugal
- Visa stamp allows entry
- Clock starts on residence permit application

### Step 4: Residence Permit (SEF/AIMA)
- Book appointment (can be long wait)
- Submit documents again
- Biometrics
- Receive residence card

### Step 5: Renewals
- First permit: Usually 2 years
- Subsequent: 3 years
- After 5 years: Permanent residence or citizenship eligible

## Timeline Reality

| Stage | Duration |
|-------|----------|
| Document preparation | 1-2 months |
| Consulate processing | 2-4 months |

**Note**: Delays are common. Build buffer time.

## Tips for Success

### Do
- Start document gathering early
- Use immigration lawyer (especially first time)
- Get NIF before applying
- Have documents properly apostilled
- Over-document your income
- Keep copies of everything

### Don't
- Apply without complete documents
- Underestimate processing times
- Assume rules haven't changed
- Enter on tourist visa planning to switch (risky)
- Miss appointment deadlines

---

## After Arrival: Path Forward

| Milestone | Timeline | What Happens |
|-----------|----------|--------------|
| Residence permit | Year 0 | Legal residence, work rights |
| Renewal | Year 2 | Continue status |
| Permanent residence | Year 5 | No more renewals needed |
| Citizenship eligible | Year 5 | Can apply if qualified |

### Citizenship Requirements
- 5 years legal residence
- Portuguese language (A2 level)
- Clean criminal record
- Ties to Portugal
- Application process: 1-2 years
