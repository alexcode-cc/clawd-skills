# IELTS Exam Configuration

## Test Types

### Academic
- **Who needs it:** University applicants, professional registration (doctors, nurses)
- **Writing Task 1:** Describe a graph, chart, table, or diagram (150+ words)
- **Reading passages:** Academic texts from journals, books, newspapers

### General Training
- **Who needs it:** Immigration (Canada, Australia, NZ), work experience programs
- **Writing Task 1:** Write a letter (formal/semi-formal/informal, 150+ words)
- **Reading passages:** Everyday texts — ads, manuals, notices, workplace documents

## Module Structure

| Module | Duration | Questions | Format |
|--------|----------|-----------|--------|
| Listening | 30 min (+10 transfer) | 40 | 4 recordings, various question types |
| Reading | 60 min | 40 | 3 passages, various question types |
| Writing | 60 min | 2 tasks | Task 1 (20 min), Task 2 (40 min) |
| Speaking | 11-14 min | 3 parts | Face-to-face interview |

## Listening Details

**4 Sections:**
1. Everyday social conversation (2 speakers)
2. Everyday social monologue (1 speaker)
3. Educational/training context (up to 4 speakers)
4. Academic lecture/monologue (1 speaker)

**Question Types:**
- Multiple choice
- Matching
- Plan/map/diagram labeling
- Form/note/table completion
- Sentence completion
- Short answer

## Reading Details

**Academic:** 3 long passages (2,150-2,750 words total)
**General Training:** 3 sections (short texts → longer text → long text)

**Question Types:**
- True/False/Not Given
- Yes/No/Not Given
- Matching headings
- Matching information
- Matching features
- Sentence completion
- Summary completion
- Multiple choice
- Short answer

## Writing Details

### Task 1 (20 min, 150+ words)
| Academic | General Training |
|----------|-----------------|
| Graph, chart, table, process diagram | Formal letter (complaint, request) |
| Compare, contrast, summarize trends | Semi-formal letter (to someone you know slightly) |
| Report style, impersonal | Informal letter (to friend/family) |

### Task 2 (40 min, 250+ words)
Both Academic and GT: Essay responding to a point of view, argument, or problem.
- Agree/disagree
- Discuss both views
- Advantages/disadvantages
- Problem/solution
- Two-part question

## Speaking Details

| Part | Duration | Format |
|------|----------|--------|
| Part 1 | 4-5 min | Introduction + familiar topics (home, work, studies) |
| Part 2 | 3-4 min | Long turn: 1 min prep, 2 min monologue on cue card |
| Part 3 | 4-5 min | Discussion: abstract questions related to Part 2 topic |

## Band Score Scale

| Band | Skill Level | Description |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| 9 | Expert | Full operational command |
| 8 | Very Good | Occasional inaccuracies |
| 7 | Good | Handles complexity, some inaccuracies |
| 6 | Competent | Generally effective, some errors |
| 5 | Modest | Partial command, many errors |
| 4 | Limited | Basic competence in familiar situations |
| 3 | Extremely Limited | Only general meaning conveyed |
| 2 | Intermittent | Great difficulty understanding |
| 1 | Non-User | No ability beyond isolated words |

## Score Calculation

**Listening & Reading:** Raw score (out of 40) converted to band
| Raw Score | Band |
|-----------|------|
| 39-40 | 9.0 |
| 37-38 | 8.5 |
| 35-36 | 8.0 |
| 33-34 | 7.5 |
| 30-32 | 7.0 |
| 27-29 | 6.5 |
| 23-26 | 6.0 |

**Writing & Speaking:** Examiner assessment on 4 criteria each
- Scores averaged and rounded to nearest 0.5

**Overall Band:** Average of 4 sections, rounded to nearest 0.5

## Test Logistics

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Registration | Online via British Council, IDP, or IELTS website |
| Formats | Computer-delivered (results in 3-5 days) or Paper-based (13 days) |
| Cost | ~$245-$310 USD (varies by country) |
| Score Validity | 2 years |
| Retake | No waiting period (can book next available date) |

## One Skill Retake (OSR)

- Available within 60 days of original test
- Retake ONE section only (Listening, Reading, Writing, OR Speaking)
- New overall band calculated with new section score
- Must be same test type (Academic or GT)
- Costs less than full retake
- **Best for:** When one section drags overall score below target
